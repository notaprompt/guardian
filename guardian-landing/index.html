The Problem

You already know what's happening.
That's the problem.

■

Your mind doesn't have an off switch. Every insight propagates. Every unresolved thread stays resident. Every loop re-derives from scratch because nothing persists outside your head.

■

You've tried the tools. Note apps that add organizational overhead. Chat wrappers that forget everything. Second-brain systems that require you to be the librarian of your own thoughts.

■

The cost isn't just time. It's substrate. Every thought held internally, every loop re-run, every insight re-derived is metabolic wear on the very infrastructure that makes you you.

Guardian exists because you shouldn't have to choose between
the depth of your thinking and the longevity of the architecture that produces it.

The Foundation

Grounded in theory.
Built on TRIM.

The Topological-Recursive Identity Model makes a specific, falsifiable prediction: individuals who consistently externalize recursive structure — systematic logging, structured AI interaction, retrievable written architecture — should show measurably reduced substrate wear compared to those who hold equivalent depth internally.

Guardian is that externalized structure made literal. Every feature exists to answer one question: does this reduce the metabolic cost of maintaining recursive structure internally?

If a feature does not serve that function, it does not ship.
