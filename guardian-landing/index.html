The Problem

Every model call is an inference boundary.
Nothing controls what crosses it.

■

Your prompts carry context you didn't intend to share. File paths, names, project details -- sent to cloud models with no inspection layer between you and the API.

■

Chat sessions vanish. Context resets. Every tool you use talks to a model, but nothing remembers what was sent, what was returned, or what was learned.

■

The inference boundary is the most important surface on your machine. Guardian puts a control plane there: intercept, scrub, route, log, remember.

Guardian sits at every inference boundary on your machine.
Local pipeline. Full provenance. Nothing leaves without your knowledge.

How It Works

Local-first.
Provable by default.

Guardian runs entirely on your machine. SQLite for storage. Full-text search for retrieval. The control plane has no cloud dependency. Cloud providers handle inference -- Guardian handles everything else.

The pipeline is modality-agnostic: intercept the request, scrub sensitive content, inject relevant context, forward to the provider, rehydrate the response, log everything. Chat was the first modality. It won't be the last.

Every feature answers one question: does this give you visibility and control at the inference boundary? If not, it doesn't ship.
